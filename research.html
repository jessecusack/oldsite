My Research

Mixing in the Samoan Passage

The Samoan Passage has been identified as an important choke point in the global overturning circulation. Dense waters moving through the Passage flow over sills and seamounts leading to the generation of intense turbulence. I have been investigating the long and short-term variability of turbulence from mooring observations. Despite fluctuations in the transport of water into the Passage, the amount of turbulence generated around sills remains remarkably constant. There appears to always be sufficient shear at the interface of the fast flowing layer to generate turbulence. This result stands in contrast other areas in the abyssal ocean where turbulence is highly intermittent.

Energy Transfers Between Mesoscale Eddies and the Internal Wave Field

Both mesoscale eddies and internal waves have been observed almost everywhere in the ocean. It has been suggested that eddies may lose energy to internal waves which eventually break and generate turbulent mixing. I am working to quantify the amount of energy loss using mooring observations in the Southern Ocean.
