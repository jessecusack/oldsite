My Research

Samoan Passage

Dense waters moving through the Samoan Passage are forced over sills and seamounts leading to the generation of intense turbulence. I have been investigating the long and short term variability of turbulence from mooring observations.

Diapycnal and Isopycnal Mixing Experiment in the Southern Ocean (DIMES)

DIMES was long term project designed to study mixing processes in the Southern Ocean. I participed in the last of 11 cruises to sample the dispersion of a chemical tracer released many years previously at the start of the project. Most of my energy was spent using data from other parts of the project to investigate lee waves and wave - eddy interactions, two of the processes thought to be a precursor to small scale turbulent mixing.
